Event Format

A riders ultimate goal is to be the champion at the end of the season. This is achieved by scoring points in the main event race, however, they have to earn their place on the gate through the following stages

Qualifying

Many riders from across the world dream of being a supercross champion, but there are only 22 spots on the gate. So, the selection process starts with a timed qualifying session. At this stage the riders on track are not battling for position directly, but through setting a fast lap time. The fastest 40 riders progress to the night show.

Heat Races

The night show begins with 2 heat races for each class (20 riders in each) where they battle for position as only the top 9 will get a transfer straight to the main event. Gate pick order is determined by finishing position so it is crucial for the riders to fight for every position they can get in order to have a better gate pick for the main. This could be the difference between them getting a good start or being buried in the pack with a lot of work to do to score good points.

Last Chance Qualifier (LCQ)

The riders outside of the top 9 in the heat races go into the LCQ, which as the name suggests, is the final opportunity to transfer into the main event. It's common to see some ambitious overtaking attempts here as riders are desperate for championship points and extra prize money awarded for being in the main.

Main Event

With all racing so far leading to this point, tensions are high as the gate drops. Expect to see riders pushing themselves to the limit in the battle for championship points. There's 17 rounds in the championship, so it is crucial for riders to get consistantly good results over the course of the season in order to be in contention for the title come the final stop in Vegas.
